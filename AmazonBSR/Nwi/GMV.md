# Amazon Global GMV Report by Country & Category

Data Period: 2024–2025 | Sources: Statista, ECDB, Marketplace Pulse, Similarweb, Amazon 10-K

Executive Summary

Amazon's total global GMV surpassed $830 billion in 2025, nearly tripling from $277 billion seven years prior. The United States remains the dominant market, accounting for over half of global GMV. However, international markets — particularly the UK, Germany, and Canada — are growing at 2–4× the US rate, signaling a strategic shift toward global diversification.

Appendix A: Metric Definitions

| Metric                 | Definition                                                                        | Who Reports It |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| 1P Net Sales / Revenue | Revenue Amazon keeps from selling its own inventory (Amazon buys low, sells high) | Amazon 10-K, Statista   |
| 3P GMV                 | Total value of goods sold by independent third-party sellers on the marketplace   | Marketplace Pulse, ECDB |
| Total GMV              | Combined 1P + 3P total value of all goods sold                                    | Similarweb, ECDB        |
| E-commerce Revenue     | Amazon's total take: 1P revenue + 3P commissions + fulfillment fees               | Amazon 10-K             |

The core difference: When a third-party seller sells a $100 product, Amazon earns ~$15 in commission but the full $100 counts as GMV. "Revenue" and "GMV" are measuring fundamentally different things.

Data Limitations & Disclaimer

• GMV figures vary by source (1P net sales vs total marketplace GMV vs third-party GMV only). Methodological differences can produce 2–3× divergences for the same market.
• Country-level category breakdowns outside the US and Germany are sparse; most are inferred from partial disclosures, analyst reports, and third-party research.
• Paywalled Statista data limits precision for France, Italy, Spain, India, Mexico, Brazil, and Australia.
• All figures are best estimates as of Q1 2026, compiled from publicly available sources.
