# Aged Inventory Surcharges

Storage fees that kick in when inventory sits in Amazon's fulfillment centers beyond a safe dwell threshold. Part of Amazon's push to incentivize inventory velocity and discourage overstocking.

## How It Works

Inventory in FBA accrues storage fees monthly. Aged inventory surcharges are **additional per-unit fees** that apply once inventory exceeds certain age thresholds. The longer it sits, the steeper the surcharge.

## 2026 vs 2025 — The Shift

| Period | 2025 Threshold | 2026 Threshold |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| Safe zone | 0–270 days | 0–180 days |
| Surcharge starts | 271 days | **181 days** |
| Critical threshold | 365+ days | 366+ days |

Amazon moved the start of aged inventory surcharges **90 days earlier** — from 271 days to 181 days. Inventory that was previously "fine" is now in the watch zone.

## 2026 Tiers

| Dwell Time | Zone | Surcharge |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| 0–180 days | Safe | None |
| 181–270 days | Watch | Tier 1 |
| 271–365 days | High Risk | Tier 2 (steeper) |
| 366+ days | Critical | Per-unit minimum enforced |

## Why 181 Days Matters Now

If your product typically takes 120 days to sell through, you're in the clear. But if you're approaching 150 days now, you have roughly **30 days** before surcharges start — not the 120 days you might have assumed under 2025 rules.

**Current action threshold:** Anything at 150+ days needs a decision **now** — not next quarter.

**Decision options:**
- **Liquidation** — Sell excess inventory to a liquidation buyer at a steep discount. Better than paying storage + surcharge.
- **Removal order** — Request Amazon ship inventory back to you (or dispose). Costly but recovers some units.
- **Promotional pricing** — Temporary price cut to move inventory faster.
- **AWD enrollment** — Moving inventory to [[Amazon AWD]] pauses the FBA aging clock and waives the surcharge.

## Q4 Multiplier Effect

Storage fees already triple during **October–December** for standard-size items. Aged inventory surcharges **stack on top** of the elevated storage rate during peak season.

Inventory that drifts into the watch zone in September hits the aged surcharge window during Q4 — when you're already paying 3× storage. The carrying cost during Q4 alone can exceed any near-term sales benefit.

## Relationship to Low-Inventory Fees

Note: these are **two separate fees**.

| Fee | Trigger | Evaluation Level | 2026 Change |
|-----|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Low-inventory fee** | Stock below 28 days supply | Per [[FNSKU]] | Now per-FNSKU not per-ASIN |
| **Aged inventory surcharge** | Inventory sitting 181+ days | Per FNSKU | Clock moved up 90 days |

A product can trigger **both simultaneously** — low stock AND aging inventory — if a slow-selling variant sits too long while other variants run out.
