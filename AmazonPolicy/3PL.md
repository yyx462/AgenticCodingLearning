# 3PL (Third-Party Logistics)

A third-party logistics company that handles storage, picking, packing, and shipping on behalf of sellers. In the Amazon context, a 3PL typically receives your bulk inventory, performs FBA prep (labels, poly-bagging, FNSKU labeling), and can split your freight across multiple [[FC (Fulfillment Center)|FC destinations]].

## What a 3PL Does for FBA Sellers

```
You → Ship bulk to 3PL → 3PL does FBA prep → 3PL splits to 5+ Amazon FCs → Amazon fulfills orders
```

Typical services:
- **FBA Prep** — Labeling, bundling, poly-bagging, FNSKU application
- **Inbound splits** — Receive one freight shipment, forward to multiple FCs
- **Storage** — Buffer inventory management
- **Cross-docking** — Minimal handling, fast throughput
- **Returns processing** — Handle Amazon returns on your behalf

## Why 3PL Matters for Placement Fees

Amazon's $0 placement fee requires 5+ FC destinations. Most sellers can't do this themselves — shipping separate LTL/FTL loads to 5+ FCs is operationally complex and expensive.

A capable 3PL solves this: you ship once to the 3PL, they distribute across your required FC network using their carrier relationships — typically at **$0.30–$0.50/unit** instead of the $0.60+/unit minimal split [[Inbound Placement Fee]].

**Example savings:**
```
5,000 units, large standard
Minimal split (direct to Amazon):  $0.60/unit = $3,000
3PL split (5+ destinations):      $0.40/unit = $2,000
Savings per shipment:               $1,000
At 12 shipments/year:               $12,000/year
```

## 3PL vs Amazon AWD

| Factor | 3PL | [[Amazon AWD]] |
|--------|-----|---------------|
| [[Inbound Placement Fee]] | $0 (at 5+ splits) | $0 |
| Low-inventory fee waiver | No | Yes |
| Q4 storage cap | No | Yes |
| FBA prep included | Usually | No |
| Auto-replenish | No | Yes |
| Monthly minimums | Usually 100–500 units | Varies |
| Best for | Sellers already using 3PL; those needing FBA prep | High-volume, automated replenishment |

## Key Terms

- **[[FNSKU]]** — Amazon's SKU identifier. 3PLs apply these during FBA prep — label accuracy is critical to avoid [[Inbound Defect Fees]].
- **[[Inbound Defect Fee]]** — Charge for misrouted, late, or abandoned shipments. Up 30% in 2026. Good 3PLs eliminate this through proper prep and routing.
- **[[FBA Prep]]** — Preparation work required before Amazon accepts inventory: labeling, packaging,hazmat checks. Most 3PLs offer this as a service.

## Choosing a 3PL for Amazon FBA

Key criteria:
- Experience with FBA requirements (ISTA-6, FNSKU labeling, hazmat)
- Multi-destination split capability (ask for FC coverage map)
- Location relative to your supplier/port (reduces inbound freight cost)
- Monthly minimums and per-handling fees
- Integration with your inventory management system
